Fighting Back

Political Affairs Committee, AAP

It is no secret that the results of the recently completed elections in Punjab, Goa and Delhi were not to our expectations. As an ambitious upstart in the dirty world of Indian politics, the Aam Aadmi Party – the party that you have a stake in as much as anyone else – has always aimed high.

But in the long arc of history, this is just a blip. We have to remind ourselves how far we have come from where we began. In the last 2 years alone, we have formed a government in Delhi with a historic mandate of 67 out of 70, we have won 22 Assembly seats and become the Opposition party in Punjab, and we now have 48 councillors in Delhi’s municipal corporations.

This also means that we have a responsibility of delivering on the dreams and aspirations of crores of people and we are focused on doing exactly that. We will learn from our mistakes, and we will move forward to strengthen this movement.

But the last few weeks of unsparing attacks, unsubstantiated allegations and institutional witch-hunting of AAP reminds what exactly we are battling against. We are fighting a world where public institutions are used as pawns to achieve political ends and where a deeply biased media isn’t interested in uncovering the truth.

But that won’t deter us from following our path. Starting today, we will communicate regularly and directly with you and we promise to listen carefully to what you have to say. So, as we fight back, we ask you to keep your faith and march with us, every step of the way.

Can EVMs be hacked?

Watch AAP MLA Saurabh Bharadwaj show live demo of EVM tampering in Delhi Assembly

The sanctity of the election process is the bedrock of Indian democracy. Over the last few years, and especially in recent months, the faith in this process has been shaken due to serious allegations of tampering the EVMs. While AAP was the first to express these concerns, recently, 16 opposition parties have made allegations about tampering - an unprecedented event in the last 19 years since the EVMs were first introduced. The most important question here is not whether EVMs were tampered with in any specific election, but whether the EVMs can be tampered at all or not. To demonstrate to ECI that they can be hacked, AAP MLA Saurabh Bhardwaj showed a historic demo on the floor of Delhi assembly on 9 May.
